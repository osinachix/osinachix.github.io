Machine Learning-Based Optimization of Wireless Charging for Mobile Robot Platforms

Research Completed Python, PyTorch, TensorFlow, Gazebo, MATLAB, ANSYS

Abstract

Developed a novel framework leveraging Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), Genetic Algorithms (GAs), and Reinforcement Learning (RL) to optimize Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) systems for Mobile Robot Platforms (MRPs). The research addressed challenges of misalignment, environmental variability, and energy inefficiencies through a comprehensive simulation-based architecture.

Key Results

  • 18-22% reduction in energy loss
  • 21% decrease in charging time
  • 15% improvement in alignment stability
  • Enhanced misalignment tolerance

Technical Approach

CNN Architecture

Spatial pattern analysis for misalignment detection

RNN with LSTM

Temporal dependency modeling

Genetic Algorithms

Coil design optimization

Deep Q-Network

Reinforcement learning for adaptive control

Methodology

  • Simulation Environment: Gazebo/ROS for MRP movement simulation, MATLAB/Simulink for control systems, ANSYS Maxwell for electromagnetic analysis
  • Data Collection: Real-time sensor data including position, coil alignment, battery SoC, and environmental parameters
  • ML Pipeline: Comprehensive preprocessing with Kalman filtering and PCA, followed by multi-model decision layer
  • Validation: Controlled environment testing with 500W inductive charging setup compliant with SAE J2954 standards

Gender Bias in Clinical Language Models

Published Research Python, PyTorch, Transformers, BERT, ClinicalBERT, BioBERT

Abstract

Investigated gender bias in clinical AI systems by comparing domain-specific clinical Large Language Models (LLMs) with general-domain BERT models. The research examined how both types of models inherit and process gender biases from medical data, with implications for equitable healthcare AI applications.

Key Findings

  • General-domain BERT models were better at inferring gender from clinical notes
  • Specialized clinical LLMs showed reduced gender bias in symptom-related predictions
  • Bias exists in both clinical data and model interpretation
  • Importance of model selection and data preprocessing in healthcare AI

Research Contributions

  • Developed methodology for quantifying gender bias in clinical NLP tasks
  • Compared bias propagation in general vs. domain-specific language models
  • Highlighted importance of bias-aware AI development in healthcare
  • Published findings through Neuromatch Academy Impact Scholar Program

Citation

Shao, Z., Adjekwei, J. A., Safarpoor, M., Mbakamma, O.E.I. (2025). "Can AI Doctors Be Fair? Unpacking the Hidden Gender Bias in Large Language Models." Forthcoming in Micropublications - Impact Scholar Program.
