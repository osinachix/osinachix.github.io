Abstract

Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) is emerging as a transformative technology for Mobile Robot Platforms (MRPs), enabling seamless and autonomous energy replenishment compared to conventional plug-in charging. However, challenges such as misalignment, environmental variability, and energy inefficiencies limit its scalability.

This research proposes a novel framework leveraging Machine Learning (ML) techniques‚Äîspecifically Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), Genetic Algorithms (GAs), and Reinforcement Learning (RL)‚Äîto optimize WPT systems for MRPs. A comprehensive simulation-based architecture is introduced, integrating real-time sensing, data preprocessing, ML-driven decision-making, and adaptive control.

Experimental results demonstrate significant improvements, including an 18‚Äì22% reduction in energy loss, a 21% decrease in charging time, and enhanced misalignment tolerance. These advancements establish a robust foundation for scalable, efficient, and intelligent WPT systems, paving the way for sustainable MRP operations in dynamic environments.

Research Methodology

1. System Architecture Design

Developed a four-layer architecture for real-time, adaptive charging optimization:

Sensing Layer

LiDAR, ultrasonic, and Hall-effect sensors for real-time data collection

Preprocessing Layer

Kalman filtering and PCA for noise reduction and feature extraction

ML Decision Layer

CNN, RNN, GA, and RL models for parameter optimization

Control Layer

Real-time adjustment of WPT parameters via microcontroller

2. Machine Learning Model Development

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

Designed for spatial pattern analysis in sensor data with three convolutional layers and two fully connected layers. Achieved 95.46% accuracy in angular misalignment detection.

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)

Implemented with LSTM units to capture temporal dependencies in charging sessions, adapting to MRP movement patterns and environmental conditions.

Genetic Algorithms (GAs)

Optimized coil design parameters over 100 generations, evolving solutions based on energy efficiency and alignment stability fitness functions.

Reinforcement Learning (RL)

Deep Q-Network agent trained over 1,000 episodes in simulated Gazebo environment, with reward function prioritizing efficiency and alignment accuracy.

3. Experimental Setup & Validation

Comprehensive testing in controlled warehouse environment with multiple MRPs:

  • WPT System: 500W inductive charging setup operating at 85kHz (SAE J2954 compliant)
  • Testing Variables: Misalignment angles (¬±10cm laterally, ¬±5¬∞ angularly), battery SoC (10-90%), environmental noise
  • Dataset: 10,000+ charging sessions including synthetic and real-world data
  • Tools: MATLAB/Simulink, Python, Gazebo/ROS, ANSYS Maxwell, LTspice

Algorithm Performance Analysis

CNN Performance: Achieved mean squared error loss of 0.012 after 50 epochs of training on 10,000 charging sessions. Demonstrated exceptional capability in spatial pattern recognition for misalignment detection.

RL Agent Performance: The DQN agent reduced energy waste by 22% in dynamic scenarios, successfully adapting to variable MRP orientations and environmental conditions through continuous exploration and exploitation.

GA Optimization: Evolved optimal coil configuration featuring 20-turn circular coil with 30cm diameter, maximizing coupling efficiency and charging stability over 100 generations.
